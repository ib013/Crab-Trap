# Crab-Trap

Microplastics, plastic particles smaller than 5mm, contaminate water sources worldwide, yet most treatment systems cannot remove them, especially in static water bodies like ponds and tanks. This project tested whether chitosan-coated iron oxide nanoparticles (Fe₃O₄), directed by an external neodymium magnet, could effectively capture and remove microplastics from contaminated water. Chitosan, derived from crustacean shells, binds to negatively charged microplastics through electrostatic attraction. In 21 trials across three nanoparticle concentrations (100, 200, and 400 mg/L) and four contact times (5, 10, 15, and 25 minutes), the optimal condition of 400 mg/L with approximately 15 minutes of mixing achieved a mean removal efficiency of 82.5% (±3.1%), exceeding the hypothesis threshold of ≥75%, consistent with the electrostatic mechanism described by Zhang et al. (2024). Results confirmed that higher concentration and longer contact time both improve removal efficiency, with diminishing returns beyond 15 minutes. This chemical-free, biodegradable approach shows promise as a scalable solution for microplastic removal in static water environments where conventional treatment fails.
